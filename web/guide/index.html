How Wand Maestro works

You conduct. A room full of phones becomes your orchestra.

The short version: your laptop is the orchestra's brain and its speaker. Phones join over Wi-Fi and each becomes an instrument. You hold a wand (your hand on a webcam, a phone, or the real one) and your gestures shape what everyone plays — all kept in time by the laptop.

1The setup

Everything talks to one central program running on your laptop. The wand sends your motion in; the laptop decides the music and tells each phone exactly what to play and when.

Why not Bluetooth? Bluetooth is point-to-point, tops out at a handful of devices, and browsers can't stream synced audio over it. The QR code just opens a link (it encodes your laptop's address on the network). The real connection is a WebSocket over Wi-Fi, which scales to a whole room and lets the laptop time every note precisely.

3How they stay in time

If each phone just "played when it heard the message," they'd be a sloppy mess — networks jitter by tens of milliseconds. Instead every device syncs its clock to the laptop (like ping figuring out the time offset), and the laptop never says "play now" — it says "play this note at time T," a fraction of a second ahead. Each phone counts down to T on its own synced clock, so they all fire together.

The six options it chooses between:

Lower imitation — a harmony under the tune Contrary motion — moves opposite the tune Sustained chord — a held pad Delayed echo — repeats last bar Rhythmic — a busy line Rest — silence
big / fast → busy gentle → calm pad twist → contrary raise / lower → octave up / down

Changes land on the next bar (~2 seconds), so conduct a beat ahead — like a real conductor cueing what's coming.

You must "grab" to conduct. Pinch thumb + index (webcam) or press & hold the screen (phone) to grab. The motion you make during the grab is the gesture, read the moment you release. Nothing happens until you grab.

Two tips. Up/down (the octave move) is reliable on the webcam — it tracks your hand's real height — but rough on the phone; there, lean on speed and twist. And to learn the sounds fast, open the Control Room and click the accompaniment buttons to hear each one before you try to gesture it.

All three send the same kind of data, so you can build and demo with your hand today and drop the real wand in later with zero changes.

6How to run it

  1. Start the server on the laptop: python server/main.py
  2. Open the orchestra on the laptop: the stage (localhost:8080) → Tap to start. The laptop plays the music and shows a QR.
  3. Conduct: click webcam wand on the laptop, or scan the stage QR with a phone (same Wi-Fi; tap through the one-time security warning).
  4. Want more instruments? Point extra phones at the stage QR's section link — each becomes its own instrument and the laptop hands the sound off to them.

7The editor (drive it by hand)

No wand yet, or the model isn't trained? The editor is a control room where you drive everything manually: press play, change tempo, force which accompaniment plays (test each of the six by hand), and assign an instrument to each phone. It's the fastest way to explore and demo while the wand and models are still being built.
